Overview

Three rooms, three questions, one future city. Participants rotate between rooms in 25-minute sessions, using a mix of digital prompts, hands-on design, and open listening and conversation to imagine Newcastle in 2050.

How each room works

  • Reflect — a screen prompt for quick digital input (Mentimeter via QR).
  • Build — table activities with maps, tokens, cards and stickers.
  • Dialogue — a provocative wall prompt with sticky notes and voting dots.

The three rooms

Room 1 · 🚲🚃

Transport & Mobility

Q205
Core question

How will we move through, in and out of Newcastle in 2050?

Focus

Encourage thinking at both scales — everyday usability and big-picture infrastructure. Push on accessibility, reduced car dependence, regional links, tourism, and how transport shapes the feel of city life.

Framing points to drop in:

  • What transport changes would most improve daily life for residents?
  • How could the CBD connect to the wider region, airport, and growth areas?
  • What would make Newcastle feel innovative, accessible, well-connected by 2050?
Build · the tables

Table Map #1 — Newcastle CBD.

Table Map #2 — NSW Regional.

Participants place 3D transport tokens on the maps to show where future infrastructure could develop. Pins and string for routes. Sticky notes / comment cards for short ideas.

Reflect · screen prompt
Screen · Mentimeter What would make Newcastle a world-class transport city?

20-minute timer per rotation. QR code on screen links to live input.

Dialogue · wall prompt
Wall What if Newcastle's CBD had no roads?

Whiteboard markers / sticky notes + green & yellow voting dots for responses.

Dialogue card prompts
  • What do you see as the advantages and disadvantages?
  • Who do you see as benefitting most?
  • Who needs to be in this conversation?
Data capture
  • Screen: Mentimeter text input.
  • Table maps: photos of 3D token placements, stickers and notes.
  • Wall prompt: sticky note / whiteboard responses with green & yellow voting dots.
  • Conversation: optional audio recording (phone voice notes) for transcription.
Room 2 · 🌿🏃

Health & Wellbeing

Q210
Core question

How will we live well in the city in 2050?

Focus

Push thinking beyond healthcare toward the daily environments that shape wellbeing. Who does the city currently support well? Where's the friction? What investments would make Newcastle healthier and more inclusive?

Framing points to drop in:

  • Health is shaped by daily environment as much as healthcare systems.
  • Access to nature improves mental health and longevity.
  • Walkable cities reduce chronic disease.
  • Social connection is a major predictor of wellbeing.
  • Urban isolation and sedentary lifestyles are growing risks.
Build · the tables

Table Map #1 — Newcastle Area.

  • Mark places that support wellbeing
  • Draw routes (walks, daily movement)
  • Sketch a "healthy day in Newcastle"

Planning Board #2 — Community: what makes a healthy community?

  • Identify areas of culture / community
  • Discuss who is missing or underserved

Tokens (limited number, for voting priorities):

Green Space Cultural Space Community Hubs Exercise Space Youth Spaces Accessibility

Participants can also add their own responses onto blank cards / sticky notes and vote on those too — encourage this.

Reflect · screen prompt
Screen · Mentimeter What questions do we need to ask to live well in Newcastle in 2050?

20-minute timer per rotation. QR code on screen links to live input.

Dialogue · wall prompt
Wall How much of our wellbeing and health is our own personal responsibility?

Whiteboard markers / sticky notes + green & yellow voting dots for responses.

Dialogue card prompts
  • What do you think "designed first for wellbeing" means?
  • If wellbeing came first, what is one everyday thing that would need to change?
  • How might a "wellbeing-first" city change how a typical day unfolds for someone?
Data capture
  • Screen: Mentimeter text input.
  • Table map: photos of stickers and notes on the map.
  • Planning board: photos of voting tokens and notes.
  • Wall prompt: sticky note / whiteboard responses with green & yellow voting dots.
  • Conversation: optional audio recording (phone voice notes) for transcription.
Room 3 · 🪩🎸

Night-Time Culture & Economy

QG01
Core question

How will we experience the city after dark in 2050?

Focus

Widen the frame beyond bars and restaurants — live music, arts, festivals, transport, safety, accessibility, late-night activity, and who the city is designed for after dark.

Framing points to drop in:

  • Newcastle's night-time culture has a rich, complex history.
  • The city is actively trying to rebuild and diversify its night economy.
  • Night culture = live music, theatre, food, festivals, public life, transport, creative experiences.
  • A great night city works for many kinds of people, not just one.
Build · the tables

Table Map #1 — Newcastle CBD.

  • Use persona cards: where do these people go, what do they do?
  • Use stickers / sticky notes to imagine the night culture and economy in 2050

Planning Board #2 — Night Program Board: what should exist at night in Newcastle?

  • Night swimming, food precincts, silent spaces…
  • Draw up your own program / vision for night culture

Tokens (limited number, for voting priorities):

Live Music Night Markets All-Ages Safe Transport Public Events Creative Spaces

Participants can also add their own responses onto blank cards / sticky notes and vote on those too — encourage this.

Reflect · screen prompt
Screen · Mentimeter What does Newcastle look like after dark in 2050?

20-minute timer per rotation. QR code on screen links to live input.

Dialogue · wall prompt
Wall What if Newcastle after dark was treated as shared public space, not just an entertainment economy?

Whiteboard markers / sticky notes + green & yellow voting dots for responses.

Dialogue card prompts
  • What night-time experiences matter to you that don't involve spending money?
  • How does moving through the city at night shape your experience?
  • What would make Newcastle feel alive after dark in 2050?
Data capture
  • Screen: Mentimeter text input.
  • Table map: photos of persona-experiences and stickers.
  • Planning board: photos of voting tokens, notes and programs.
  • Wall prompt: sticky note / whiteboard responses with green & yellow voting dots.
  • Conversation: optional audio recording (phone voice notes) for transcription.

Conversational moves

Use these when a discussion stalls, sharpens, or needs one more turn. They work in any room.

Can you say a bit more about what you mean by…?
Something you said makes me think about…
What I'm hearing you say is…
What makes that important to you?
Who benefits — and who might be left out?
What would need to be true for that to work?

Capture before the break

After each session — 2 minutes

  • Photograph the table maps — token placements, stickers, notes, routes.
  • Photograph the planning board — voting tokens, written ideas, any participant-added priorities.
  • Photograph the wall prompt with all sticky-note responses and green / yellow voting dots.
  • Save or export the Mentimeter screen before resetting for the next group.
  • Label the photos with room + session number (e.g. Health-S2) so we can sort them later.

Then reset the room for the next group: clear wall notes (once photographed), re-bowl the tokens, fresh sticky notes, blank cards ready for new contributions.

Record participant conversations

Great conversations are happening in every room — capturing them is one of the most valuable things we can do. Rode microphones will be available; grab one and use it.

  • Ask first. "Happy for me to record a couple of minutes of this?" — most people will say yes.
  • Keep it short. 3–5 minutes is plenty. One clear thought beats ten minutes of meandering.
  • Follow the heat. When a table lights up — a story, a disagreement, a vivid idea — that's the moment to record.
  • Prompt if needed: "Say a bit more about that." · "What would need to be true for that?" · "Who would that be for?"
  • Back up audio to your phone / cloud at each break. Label file as Room-Session-ShortTopic.

If Rode mics are in use, a phone voice memo is a perfectly good backup. Prioritise getting the conversation recorded over getting the perfect recording.
